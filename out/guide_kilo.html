DEFINITIONS OF KILOMINX PIECES
THE PARTS:
CORNER PIECES
PIECES WITH THREE (3) COLORS. THERE ARE TWENTY (20) CORNER PIECES — these are the ONLY pieces you move. The whole puzzle is corners!
CENTER PIECES
PIECES WITH ONE (1) COLOR. THERE ARE TWELVE (12), ONE ON EACH FACE. THEY DO NOT MOVE and show the color of their face.
A kilominx is a megaminx with no edge pieces — just corners and centers. It has 12 faces and 12 colors; each color is on exactly one face. WHITE and GRAY are on opposite faces — you start with the white face on top and finish with the gray one on the bottom. (Use the face colors of YOUR puzzle; the steps are the same.)
1
EACH FACE OF THE KILOMINX IS REPRESENTED BY A LETTER
U= Up Face — the top face
F= Front Face — the face facing you, below the top
R= Right Face — the face just right of the front
L= Left Face — the face just left of the front
D= Down Face — the face below and between F and R
A letter with an "i" after it means an inverted (counter-clockwise) turn of that face. A letter alone means a clockwise turn (as you look at that face). Every turn is one click — one fifth of the way around.
VERY IMPORTANT
The pictures show exactly which face moves and which way. Hold your kilominx to match the picture before each sequence. Dark gray on a picture means the color there does not matter.

STAGE 2:
SOLVE THE WHITE CORNERS
Holding Your Kilominx: Hold your kilominx with the WHITE center on top (U). You will fill the five corners around the white face, each with white on top and its two side colors matching the side centers.
Your Goal
Tips:
  • Find a white corner in the bottom of the puzzle. Park it directly below the slot it belongs in by turning the bottom faces.
  • Then repeat the Righty move below — (R)i (D)i (R) (D) — until the corner pops up into place with white on top. Repeat 1, 2, or 3 times.
  • Turning the bottom faces never disturbs corners already finished on top, so work one corner at a time.

Holding Your Kilominx: Keep white on top. Now fill the ring of five corners just below the white ones — each has two side colors and no white and no gray.

  • Park the corner you want directly below its slot, then repeat Righty until it seats. Same move as the white corners, one row lower.

STAGE 6:
LAST LAYER: TWIST THE CORNERS
Holding Your Kilominx: Still GRAY on top. Every corner is in the right place — now twist each one so gray faces up. This uses the Righty move you already know.
Solved!
Tips:
  • Look at the front-right corner. Repeat Righty until its gray sticker faces up. The lower part will look scrambled — that is normal, it fixes itself.
  • Now turn ONLY the gray top face to bring the next un-twisted corner to the front-right, and repeat. Do not turn anything else.
  • When the last corner twists up, a final turn of the gray face snaps the whole puzzle solved. You did it!

Fun Facts!
A kilominx is sometimes called a "corners-only megaminx." Take a megaminx, throw away every edge piece, and this is what is left.
It still has twenty corner pieces in a dozen colors — plenty of ways to scramble, but always the same secret to solve.
Every solve in this booklet was checked, move by move, on a computer model of the puzzle — so if you follow the pictures exactly, it always works.
The method you just learned is the same idea as the megaminx and your cube: build the white layer, drop in the middle corners with the Righty move, then finish the last layer in two small steps. Different puzzle — same secret.
Made for a young puzzler who already unlocked the cube. Happy twisting!
